Hi there! This is sparklegoose! I'm an international student from Beijing, China, currently studying Cognitive Science and Philosophy at Carnegie Mellon University.

Honeslty, before I came here, I was super conerend about my food. Food is always of extremely high priority to me, and I've heard so many terrifying things about how studying in the US will make your belly suffer with either bland food or unhealthy trash food.

...But! After staying in Pittsburgh for a while, I started to realize that I had the wrong assumeption. The food here is actually very, very good!

I've tried food from so many different countries and regions beyond the food I grew up eating. Indian, Mexican, Middel Eastern, Ethiopian(my favorite!), and oh my gosh I love them all!

And I always have fun finding commonalities between food from different regions. For example, raviolis, pieorgis and dumplings...they are deifnitely related!

Those commonalities also make me realize that the so-called cutlural boundries are deiniftely not as hard-to-conqure as they seem to be.

In Beijing we greet people with “吃了吗您内？(Chi Le Ma Nin Nei)?” It literally translated to "have you eaten?".

Well, I guess our ancestors are very wise and correct. It is true that food connects us all.
